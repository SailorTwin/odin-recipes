Bread

Recipy

Bread is bread, take it or leave it

and it is very good

Ingredients

  • 1 ⅓ cups warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 1 ½ teaspoons olive oil
  • 4 cups unbleached all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon light brown sugar
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons cornmeal
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon water

    • Steps

  1. gather all ingredients.

  2. Place 1 1/3 cups warm water, olive oil, flour, brown sugar, salt, and yeast into a bread machine in the order listed, or follow the order recommended by your manufacturer if different. Run the Dough cycle, about 90 minutes.
  3. Sprinkle cornmeal onto a cutting board. Punch down dough, turn out onto a lightly floured surface, and form into two loaves.
  4. Sprinkle cornmeal onto a cutting board. Punch down dough, turn out onto a lightly floured surface, and form into two loaves.
  5. Place loaves, seam-side down, onto the cutting board; cover with a damp cloth and let rise until doubled in volume, about 40 minutes.
  6. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  7. Beat egg and 1 tablespoon water together in a small bowl; brush over risen loaves.
  8. Use a sharp knife to quickly cut one long slash down the center of each loaf.
  9. Gently shake the cutting board to make sure the loaves are not sticking; use a spatula or pastry knife to loosen them if necessary. Slide loaves onto a baking sheet with a quick but careful motion.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven until loaves are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom, 30 to 35 minutes.
